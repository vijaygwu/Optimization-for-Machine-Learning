\chapter*{After Reading This Book, You Will...}
\thispagestyle{empty}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, itemsep=1em]
    \item \textbf{Understand why convexity guarantees global optima}---and recognize when your problems are convex, when they're not, and what that means for optimization

    \item \textbf{Implement SGD, momentum, and Adam from scratch}---not just call library functions, but understand every line of the algorithm and why it works

    \item \textbf{Choose the right optimizer for your problem}---know when Adam beats SGD, when SGD with momentum is better, and why different problems favor different approaches

    \item \textbf{Apply regularization intelligently}---understand L1 vs L2, dropout, weight decay, and early stopping as principled tools rather than arbitrary tricks

    \item \textbf{Design loss functions for different tasks}---select and customize objectives for classification, regression, generation, and beyond

    \item \textbf{Debug training using convergence theory}---diagnose why training is slow, unstable, or stuck using mathematical principles rather than guesswork

    \item \textbf{Tune learning rates with confidence}---understand learning rate schedules, warmup, and decay as consequences of convergence theory

    \item \textbf{Navigate loss landscapes conceptually}---visualize and reason about the geometry of optimization in high dimensions
\end{enumerate}

\vfill

\end{center}
